IBM Employee Attrition

  • “Kaggle Competition Dataset”
  • “HR.csv” | Original Data source: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/pavansubhasht/ibm-hr-analytics-attrition-dataset

summary of data

  • Dataset Structure: 1470 observations (rows), 35 features (variables)
  • Missing Data: Luckily for us, there is no missing data! this will make it easier to work with the dataset.
  • Data Type: We only have two datatypes in this dataset: factors and integers
  • Label : Attrition is the label in our dataset and we would like to find out why employees are leaving the organization!
  • Imbalanced dataset: 1237 (84% of cases) employees did not leave the organization while 237 (16% of cases) did leave the organization making our dataset to be considered imbalanced since more people stay in the organization than they actually leave.

1.6 Conclusion

  • According to the dataset, there appears to be no gender pay gap, as females make slightly more than males at the entry and mid-senior levels. However, at the top management and executive level, there is a significant disparity between males and females, with the male population earning more than the female population.*ANOVA TEST CONFIRMED.
  • It may be possible that the difference in pay between genders at top management and executive levels could be due to factors such as differences in education, experience, performance, or negotiation skills. Alternatively, there may be underlying biases or discrimination that contribute to the observed differences.
  • Based on the histograms and the QQ plots, both “MonthlyIncome” and “YearsAtComapny” shows right skewness and qqplot deviates form the qqline providing some indication of non-normality at the tails.
  • The variables “JobRole”, “MaritalStatus” and “OverTime” indicating strong evidence against the null hypothesis of independence. This suggests that there is a significant association among Attrition. “WorkLifeBalance” suggests that there may be a significant association between Attrition and WorkLifeBalance but the evidence is not as strong as in the previous tests.
  • Overall: Further analysis could involve examining the relationship between gender and other variables that may affect pay, such as job role, years of experience, education level, and performance metrics. It may also be useful to conduct surveys or interviews to gain insights into any potential biases or discrimination that may be influencing pay decisions.

3.6 General Recommendation to IBM

This report presents the results of a re-leveled GLM logistic model that analyzes the impact of job level position, marital status, and business travel on employee attrition among IBM employees. The findings reveal some key insights that could assist companies in reducing employee attrition and retaining their top talent.
  • The report analyzed the effects of job level position and marital status on employee attrition among IBM employees using a re-leveled GLM logistic model.
  • Employees in higher-level positions such as Top-level, Executive-level, Middle-level, and Senior-level have a significantly lower likelihood of attrition than entry-level employees.
  • On average, employees in senior-level positions are more likely to leave than middle-level positions, and top-level positions are least likely to leave than executive-level positions in the company.
  • Single employees have the highest attrition rate in the company, and divorced employees are less likely to leave than married employees.
  • Business travel has a significant impact on employee attrition. Employees who frequently go on business travel have a significantly lower likelihood of attrition than non-business travelers.
In conclusion, these findings suggest that companies can reduce employee attrition and retain their top talent by providing support and opportunities to employees in higher-level positions, considering the unique needs and motivations of employees with different marital statuses, and promoting business travel opportunities for their employees.
